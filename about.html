About

Region

The Ozarks

The Ozark Region has an expansion history going back thousands of years. In the early days of the region, Paleoindians—the first humans of the Americas—lived in the area until Europeans began to settle here in the 1800s. Throughout the late 19th century through the 20th century, farmers, loggers, and miners made their home here. There are countless structures, artifacts, and landscapes that teach and remind us of the rich history of the region.

Don't forget to explore the local treasures and activities the Ozarks have to offer!


America's First National River

In 1972, the Buffalo National River came to status as America's first national river. This beautiful river flows for 135 miles throughout Arkansas' luscious Ozark mountains and is one of the last few undammed rivers in the country. Enjoy your cruise through rushing rapids and serene pools, surrounded by towering rocky bluffs all the way down to the White River.
